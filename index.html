Primary (1°), Secondary (2°), Tertiary (3°) Amines

  • Primary: R-NH₂ (one alkyl/aryl group)
  • Secondary: R₂NH (two alkyl/aryl groups)
  • Tertiary: R₃N (three alkyl/aryl groups)
  • Quaternary ammonium salt: R₄N⁺X⁻ (four alkyl groups, positive charge)

Aliphatic vs Aromatic Amines

  • Aliphatic: CₙH₂ₙ₊₃NGeneral formula for primary aliphatic amines (e.g., methylamine, ethylamine)
  • Aromatic: Contain benzene ring (e.g., phenylamine/aniline)

⚗️ Preparation Methods - KEY EXAM TOPIC

▼

1. Primary Aliphatic Amines from Halogenoalkanes

Reagents & Conditions: Excess ammonia in ethanol, warm

R-X + 2NH₃ → R-NH₂ + NH₄⁺X⁻

Mechanism: Nucleophilic substitution

  • Step 1: NH₃ attacks δ⁺ carbon (nucleophile)
  • Step 2: Second NH₃ removes H⁺ (acts as base)
EXAM TIP: Use EXCESS ammonia to prevent further substitution to 2° and 3° amines

2. Primary Aliphatic Amines from Nitriles

Reagents & Conditions:

  • H₂ + Ni catalyst, high T & P
  • OR LiAlH₄ in dry ether
R-C≡N + 2H₂ → R-CH₂-NH₂

Type: Reduction reaction

3. Aromatic Amines (Phenylamine)

Step 1 - Nitration: Benzene + HNO₃ + H₂SO₄ → Nitrobenzene

Step 2 - Reduction: Nitrobenzene + Sn + HCl, reflux → Phenylamine

Overall: Reduction of nitro compounds

⚖️ Base Properties - FREQUENTLY EXAMINED

▼

Key Definition

Base: Substance that accepts H⁺ ions (lone pair donor)

Basicity Order (LEARN THIS!)

1° Aliphatic amines > Ammonia > 1° Aromatic amines

Explanation of Basicity Differences

Aliphatic amines (STRONGER bases):

  • Alkyl groups are electron-donating (+I effect)
  • Increases electron density on nitrogen
  • Lone pair MORE available to accept H⁺

Aromatic amines (WEAKER bases):

  • Lone pair delocalised into benzene ring
  • Decreases electron density on nitrogen
  • Lone pair LESS available to accept H⁺
EXAM TIP: Always explain basicity in terms of "availability of lone pair"

Reactions with Acids

R-NH₂ + HCl → R-NH₃⁺Cl⁻
  • Forms ionic salts (soluble in water)
  • Can be reversed with alkali: R-NH₃⁺Cl⁻ + NaOH → R-NH₂ + NaCl + H₂O

🎯 Nucleophilic Properties

▼

Key Definition

Nucleophile: Electron pair donor (due to lone pair on nitrogen)

Nucleophilic Substitution with Halogenoalkanes

Products formed in sequence:

  1. Primary amine: RX + NH₃ → RNH₂
  2. Secondary amine: RX + RNH₂ → R₂NH
  3. Tertiary amine: RX + R₂NH → R₃N
  4. Quaternary ammonium salt: RX + R₃N → R₄N⁺X⁻

Mechanism name: Nucleophilic substitution

Nucleophilic Addition-Elimination Reactions

With Acyl Chlorides

RCOCl + 2NH₃ → RCONH₂ + NH₄⁺Cl⁻ (primary amide)
RCOCl + 2R'NH₂ → RCONHR' + R'NH₃⁺Cl⁻ (secondary amide)

With Acid Anhydrides

(RCO)₂O + R'NH₂ → RCONHR' + RCOOH

Products: Amide + Carboxylic acid

Mechanism name: Nucleophilic addition-elimination

🧼 Quaternary Ammonium Salts ("Quats")

▼

Structure & Uses

  • Cationic surfactants (positive charge on nitrogen)
  • Uses: Detergents, fabric conditioners
  • Structure: Long hydrocarbon chains + ionic head
  • Soluble in water (ionic region)
  • Insoluble in water (hydrocarbon region)

📝 Exam Command Words & Mark Scheme Language

▼

When explaining basicity differences:

  • ✅ USE: "availability of lone pair"
  • ✅ USE: "electron density on nitrogen"
  • ✅ USE: "delocalisation into ring"
  • ❌ AVOID: "stronger/weaker" without explanation

When writing mechanisms:

  • ✅ CURLY ARROWS: Start from lone pair, go to δ⁺ carbon
  • ✅ SHOW: All charges and lone pairs
  • ✅ LABEL: Products clearly

Common Exam Mistakes to Avoid:

  1. Forgetting to use EXCESS ammonia in nucleophilic substitution
  2. Not explaining basicity in terms of lone pair availability
  3. Drawing straight arrows instead of curly arrows in mechanisms
  4. Forgetting the second step in nucleophilic substitution (base removal of H⁺)
  5. Not balancing equations properly (remember 2 moles of nucleophile needed)

📋 Quick Reference Equations

▼

Preparation

From halogenoalkanes: CH₃CH₂Br + 2NH₃ → CH₃CH₂NH₂ + NH₄⁺Br⁻
From nitriles: CH₃CH₂CN + 2H₂ → CH₃CH₂CH₂NH₂

Base reactions

With acid: CH₃NH₂ + HCl → CH₃NH₃⁺Cl⁻
Salt + alkali: CH₃NH₃⁺Cl⁻ + NaOH → CH₃NH₂ + NaCl + H₂O

Nucleophilic reactions

With acyl chloride: CH₃COCl + 2NH₃ → CH₃CONH₂ + NH₄⁺Cl⁻
With anhydride: (CH₃CO)₂O + CH₃NH₂ → CH₃CONHCH₃ + CH₃COOH

🎯 Key Exam Tips Summary

▼
  1. Always use EXCESS ammonia for primary amine synthesis
  2. Basicity depends on lone pair availability - learn the order!
  3. Curly arrows in mechanisms - from lone pair to δ⁺ carbon
  4. Two-step nucleophilic substitution - nucleophile attack then base removal
  5. Balance equations correctly - 2 moles nucleophile in most reactions
